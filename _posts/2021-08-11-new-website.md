## Building my portfolio

A few applications into the summer 2022 internship season and I knew my LinkedIn page wouldn't show the full picture of my capabilities. So it was time to bunker down and crank out my personal website. 

So much of my skills right now lie outside of programming. My problem solving skills, communication, and ability to see the big picture are difficult to convey in a one page feature churned through resume reader bots. Even beyond this, I wanted a way to feature the projects I had been tinkering on over the summer, and a space that could grow with me as my skills did. 

College has been up until now mostly general education in its focus, and I knew with my acceptance to Paul G. Allen's School of Engineering that my lessons were about to turn into a firehose of information I had been eager to learn for the last 2 years. My goals in expanding from business management to tech were always to broaden my understanding, to create better ideas, products, and solutions. I'm excited to see who I collaborate with, and post about future projects as I get to work with other diverse minds at University of Washington the next two years.

This website was forked from developer [Sujay Kundu](https://github.com/sujaykundu777), as an opportunity for me to practice with Git, command promt mastery, Ruby (the first language I learned), and see if I could in fact still brush off the dust and remember my HTML/css basics (I can!).
